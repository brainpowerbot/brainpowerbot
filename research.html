Research

A lot of research went into this project and this section will highlight all the big decisions taken and why we chose them.

  • Bot framework choice

Bot framework choice

Since the beginning of the semester we knew that building a chat bot framework from scratch and then still implement the features we wanted our bot to have, would be near impossible. Since this was the case a decision was made to look into frameworks that already existed.

Since this was something that could not easily be changed in the middle of a semester, we decided it would be best to take our time in deciding what framework to use. Our team knew that if we chose a framework that would end up not being what we wanted it to be, it would force us to start from scratch and lose all the work we had up to that point. Our strategy to try and pick the right platform for us was to spread out the research work and have everyone take a look at one or two frameworks and learn as much about it as possible. After this, we compiled a spreadsheet to determine which framework would best fit our needs and Dialogflow was what we ended up going with.

Facebook & Facebook API Limitations

The reason for making an entire project around Facebook is because Facebook can provide more details of relantionships between people better than any other social media platform. Facebook has more information and connections that we can then use to make our bot as helpful to our target audience as possible.

Even though Facebook is the platform with the most information that is useful to us, their access to this information is very limited. With security and privacy concerns constantly sorrounding Facebook, they have restricted public access to data from their API. With these data restrictions in place, it was very difficult getting the information we needed for this project. Instead, what we decided to do was create a test environment where we could have staged data to come up with a proof of concept for our ideas. For a full understanding of what data exactly we were not allowed access to please download our Facebook API reaserch document.

Risk management

As part of managing a project, risk management is important to consider especially for a year long project. Some risks that could affect us were put into a spreadsheet early on in the project. We then went on and used a ranking system to determine which risks were worth keeping track of. This spreadsheet is meant to be a "living document", meaning we are constantly updating it, and we were making sure that the highest ranking risks were not an issue.

Testing practices

Another thing worth tracking and keeping up to date is our testing practices. Since our bot takes in data from people using it to better itself, we had a test script to use on the bot and check expected results. An example testing checklist can be seen here. We wanted to make sure that all the work we were doing, worked how we expected it to so we constantly tested our bot this way.

We decided on having a manual test checklist instead of other forms of testing because this chat bot is very much user based. Automation testing as we made changes to the bot and deployed new code, we thought, was unecessary since most changes did not have drastic changes to the bot's behaviour. We were mainly trying to get the bot to recognize either set phrases or keywords so it knew what to do. Keeping track of a manual testing checklist is what we decided would be best for this project.
